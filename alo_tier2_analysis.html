Tier 2 Scoring Framework

Site-level methodology
Unlike Tier 1 (metro-level opportunity), Tier 2 evaluates specific retail venues on five dimensions. Each dimension is independently scored 0–10 and combined using the weights below.
Venue Quality
25%
  • National sales/sqft ranking
  • Anchor tenant caliber (Nordstrom / Neiman / Saks)
  • Physical condition & recent capex
  • Vacancy rate (<5% = 10, >15% = 2)
  • Center ownership (Simon / Westfield = premium)
Trade Area Demographics
25%
  • % HH income $150K+ within 5mi
  • Median age 28–42 (target cohort)
  • Daytime population density
  • % college-educated within 5mi
  • Nearby employer base (tech / finance / biotech)
Co-Tenancy
25%
  • Lululemon same venue (strongest signal)
  • Apple Store presence
  • Anthropologie / Free People / Aritzia
  • Luxury anchors (Nordstrom, Neiman, RH)
  • Equinox / boutique fitness within 0.25mi
  • Whole Foods within 0.5mi
Accessibility
15%
  • Parking abundance & convenience
  • Highway proximity / interchange
  • Transit score (for urban sites)
  • In-venue positioning (end-cap premium)
  • Ride-share / pedestrian access
Real Estate Economics
10%
  • Est. rent $/sqft vs. category avg
  • Occupancy cost % (target <18%)
  • Knockout: occ cost >22%
  • Lease term flexibility
  • CAM / NNN load estimate

Knockout Rules

Automatic disqualification criteria applied before scoring is considered
  • ⛔ Occupancy cost >22% of projected revenue
  • ⛔ Venue vacancy rate >15% (declining center)
  • ⛔ No Lululemon within 1mi (unless flagship-street rationale)
  • ⛔ 5-mi radius median HH income <$90K
  • ⛔ Direct Alo®-within-2mi (saturation overlap)
Result: 0 knockouts across all 21 sites. All candidates in these five gap markets cleared every threshold. The Tier 1 screening process effectively pre-filtered for favorable real estate economics — premium demographics support premium rents.
